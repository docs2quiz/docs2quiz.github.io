This walks through the whole flow once, end to end.

## 1. Create a knowledge base

On the home page, click **+ Create Knowledge**. Give it a title and select one or more files (`.pdf`, `.docx`, `.txt`, `.md`).

The knowledge base starts in **Embedding Processing** status while your documents are chunked and indexed. This can take anywhere from a few seconds to a couple of minutes depending on document size — the page polls automatically and updates when it's done.

## 2. Wait for "Ready"

Once the status badge switches to **Ready**, click the card to open it.

## 3. Enter a keyword

On the generate page you'll see your source files and how many chunks were indexed. Type a keyword or topic into the **Keyword** field — something you know appears in your documents. Not sure what's in there? Click **📊 Keyword Stats** to see the most frequent terms across your uploaded content.

## 4. Generate

Click **Generate Questions**. Questions stream in one at a time as they're generated and verified — you'll see a live progress counter. Each finished question shows its options, and a **Source Evidence** block citing exactly which document and span it was grounded in.

## 5. Export

Once you have results, use the **⬇ Export** menu to download them as PDF (print-ready), DOCX (editable), or JSON (raw data).

That's it — see [Generating Questions](/guides/generating-questions/) for everything the generate page can do, or [Knowledge Bases](/guides/knowledge-bases/) for details on managing your uploaded content.
